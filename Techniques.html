Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method that breaks work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks.




Eisenhower Matrix

Eisenhower Matrix is a technique of prioritizing of your task on the basis of their importance. It helps to find out the work which is most important and which deserve more attention.

After clicking on button: 1. The appearance (theme) of this page will be based on your system theme settings. 2. Click on the second icon after “search” to create your own eisenhower matrix in just one click.




The 2-Minute Rule

The rule is simple: If a task can be completed in two minutes or less, do it immediately. The idea is to handle quick and small tasks on the spot rather than letting them accumulate on your to-do list.




EAT Method

(Eliminate, Automate, Delegate)

"EAT" in time management stands for "Eliminate, Automate, Delegate (Tackle or Take Action)," where you assess tasks. Eliminate unnecessary ones, automate repetitive tasks, and take immediate action on those that need attention, streamlining productivity and focus.
